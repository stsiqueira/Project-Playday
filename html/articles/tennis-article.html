Tennis

How to Grip a Tennis Racket

  1. Hold the racket pointing away from you, in your dominant hand.
  2. Orient the stringed area of the racket perpendicular to the ground.
  3. Place the base knuckle of your index finger on the long flat side of the grip facing straight out to your right.
  4. Close your hand around the grip so that this same bevel of the grip goes diagonally across your palm.

How to Shoot

  1. Stand with your feet behind the baseline and toss the tennis ball into the air.
  2. As the ball travels back down, make contact by pushing it over the net and diagonally into the service box, where your opponent is.
  3. Spend time practicing this crucial stroke.

Clothing and Gear

  1. Choose a racket for your hand size, with tight strings. Lightweight rackets are preferable.
  2. The tension of the strings should be high, in order to hit the ball harder.
  3. Choose a hard surface to play on, such as wood or concrete floored courts.
  4. Wear traditional white clothes or loose fitting athletic wear, to make playing more comfortable.
